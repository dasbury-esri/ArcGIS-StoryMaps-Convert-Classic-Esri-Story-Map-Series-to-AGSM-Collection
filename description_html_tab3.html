From 2000 and 2010, the population of 

New Orleans decreased by 29%.

 

2000 total population: 484,674

 - 325,947 African Americans

 - 135,956 Whites

 - 14,826 Latinos/Hispanics

 

2010 total population: 343,829
 - 206,871 African Americans (37% loss)

 - 113,428 Whites (17% loss)

 - 18,051 Latinos/Hispanics (22% gain) 

 

Predominantly Black neighborhoods suffered the most damage and lost the most residents following Hurricane Katrina. The city did see a notable influx of Latino immigrants, who provided an important labor force in the city's revitaliztion and restoration. Click on each neighborhood for a racial breakdown of population shifts.
 
Current estimates place the 2015 population of New Orleans at about 380,000 residents, 100,000 fewer than who lived there in 2000, but around 40,000 more than in 2010. 
 

Data Source: The Data Center analysis of data from U.S. Census 2000 Summary File 1 (SF1) and U.S. Census 2010 Summary File 1 (SF1)
